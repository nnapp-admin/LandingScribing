If youre evaluating DeepScribe in 2026, one of the first questions youll have is:
How much does it cost, and is it the right choice for my clinic?

DeepScribe remains one of the most advanced AI medical scribes in the industry, but its pricing, onboarding requirements, and enterprise focus make it less suitable for smaller practices. This guide gives you a complete breakdown of DeepScribes pricing, features, pros and cons, and the best alternatives including Scribing.io, a fast-growing, affordable AI medical scribe for clinics and solo doctors.

What Is DeepScribe?

DeepScribe is a San Franciscobased AI medical scribe founded in 2017 and backed by $24 million in funding. It is used by more than 1,500 healthcare organizations across the U.S. In 2025, DeepScribe earned a KLAS Research score of 98.8/100, making it one of the top-rated ambient AI documentation platforms.

It is widely known for its:

  • transcription accuracy

  • specialty documentation

  • EHR integrations

  • coding assistance

DeepScribe is designed primarily for large clinics, hospitals, and specialty practices that handle complex documentation.

DeepScribe Features (2026)

DeepScribe offers a sophisticated ambient AI system that captures doctorpatient conversations and converts them into structured notes. Its key features include:

1. Automatic real-time note generation
Captures the conversation as it happens and produces structured notes.

2. High transcription accuracy
Trained on broad medical vocabulary and specialty terminology.

3. SOAP note automation
Integrates directly with Epic, Cerner, and athenahealth.

4. AI-powered coding suggestions
Supports ICD-10, CPT, and HCC codes for billing accuracy.

5. Specialty templates
Tailored for oncology, orthopedics, cardiology, neurology, and more.

6. Customization Studio
Clinicians can personalize note formats and workflows.

7. HIPAA-compliant security
Includes encryption, access controls, and full healthcare data protection.

8. Multi-device compatibility
Supports desktop, iPhone, and iPad.

Ideal Customer Profile for DeepScribe

DeepScribe is built for organizations that need high accuracy, deep EHR integration, and complex clinical workflows. It is ideal for:

  • large outpatient centers

  • multi-specialty provider groups

  • hospitals

  • oncology, cardiology, and surgical practices

  • practices using Epic, Cerner, or athenahealth

  • teams focused on improving billing and documentation compliance

Smaller clinics may find DeepScribe powerful but overly expensive and complex.

DeepScribe Pricing in 2026

DeepScribe does not publish its pricing online. All plans require contacting sales and scheduling a demo. However, verified industry data indicates consistent pricing across organizations.

Below is a breakdown of expected pricing.

1. Non-EHR Integrated Plan

For clinics that want AI transcription and note generation without connecting to an EHR.

Includes:

  • real-time transcription

  • AI note generation

  • basic templates

Estimated pricing:
$300$400 per provider per month

2. EHR-Integrated Plan

DeepScribes most common plan for clinics needing workflow automation.

Includes:

  • full Epic, Cerner, and athenahealth integration

  • coding suggestions

  • advanced note automation

  • workflow enhancements

Estimated pricing:
$500$650 per provider per month

EHR integration typically increases the monthly cost by $100 or more per provider.

3. Enterprise Plan

For large hospitals and multi-specialty groups.

Includes:

  • human-in-loop QA (up to 99% accuracy)

  • dedicated onboarding

  • custom integrations

  • enterprise governance support

Estimated pricing:
$700$900 per provider per month

Factors That Affect DeepScribe Pricing

DeepScribes cost varies depending on:

  • provider count

  • level of EHR integration

  • specialty complexity

  • need for human-reviewed notes

  • onboarding and training requirements

  • billing workflows

  • length of contract (annual contracts often reduce cost)

DeepScribe is generally priced toward enterprise-level organizations and specialty-heavy clinics.

Is DeepScribe Worth the Price?

DeepScribe is a strong choice if your clinic:

  • is large or multi-specialty

  • requires high accuracy

  • needs deep EHR integration

  • does heavy coding and billing

  • values enterprise support

It may not be ideal if your clinic:

  • is small or independent

  • wants transparent pricing

  • needs instant setup

  • prefers simple workflows

  • cannot justify high monthly fees

For many solo practitioners and small clinics, more affordable alternatives provide similar value at a fraction of the cost.
